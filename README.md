# Where Do I Belong?

A simple interactive narrative game prototype for a 30-40 minute class workshop activity.

## How to Run

Open `index.html` in a web browser. The game uses only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, so it does not need a server or external libraries.

## Workshop Purpose

This activity invites elementary and middle school youth to reflect on identity, memory, language, migration, and belonging through a gentle fictional story. The main character, Sasha Kim, is a fictional Koryo Saram youth living in Kazakhstan who is curious about family history and Korean culture.

The game is designed for discussion, not scoring. There are no right or wrong choices. Each choice gives a short reflection and adds a few identity words that appear on the final screen.

## Identity, Storytelling, and Ethical Engagement

The story is inspired by themes connected to the Koryo Saram community, but it does not present that community as having one single experience. Facilitators should make clear that Sasha is fictional and that real people and families have many different histories, languages, memories, and relationships to belonging.

This prototype can help students notice that identity may include:

- Family stories and memories
- Languages people speak, hear, forget, or want to learn
- Places people live now and places connected to family history
- Feelings of pride, confusion, hurt, curiosity, or connection
- Personal choice and change over time

Facilitators should handle community stories with care. Avoid stereotypes, avoid asking one story to represent everyone, and leave room for participants to share or stay quiet. The final reflection questions are meant to support respectful discussion after the game.

## Editing the Story

Most story content is in the `scenes` array inside `script.js`. To revise the prototype, edit the scene titles, text, choices, responses, or identity words there.
